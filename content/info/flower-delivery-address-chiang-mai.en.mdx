## Why written addresses are often unreliable

In Thailand, a correct-looking address can still confuse Google Maps.

Common issues include:

- **Translation differences** — Thai and English spellings of the same place rarely match Google’s labels exactly
- **Shared numbering** — several villages or projects may use similar house numbers
- **New developments** — new housing projects may not appear clearly on Maps yet
- **Vague streets** — some roads are only numbered (for example “Road 1”) with little extra context

Even when every line looks right, the driver may still need a landmark or pin to find the exact drop-off.

## How Thai addresses are structured

A typical Chiang Mai-area address includes layers that matter for navigation:

- **Amphoe (district)** — for example San Sai, Hang Dong, or Mae Rim (not only “Chiang Mai”)
- **Tambon (subdistrict)** — such as San Sai Luang or Mueang Len (ตำบลเมืองเล็น)
- **Muban / village or housing project name** — often the most important missing piece
- **Street and house number** — after the village or project name

Without the village or housing project name, many addresses stay unclear because nearby areas share similar numbering systems.

**Tip:** If you only have a house number, Moo (หมู่), subdistrict, and district, add the project name or a nearby landmark whenever you can.

## Why a Google Maps pin helps so much

A **Google Maps pin** does not have to sit on the exact front door. A nearby point that is easy to find is often enough, such as:

- the village or project entrance
- a nearby shop or convenience store
- a temple, school, or clear landmark
- the condo lobby or hotel porte-cochère

Share the pin in your order notes (or when we confirm the order in chat). Pair it with the written address so the driver has both a route and a readable destination name.

This matters even more in Chiang Mai’s heat. The longer a driver searches, the longer the bouquet stays outdoors. Car delivery usually protects flowers better than motorcycle delivery; either way, an accurate pin shortens the trip.

## Hotels, condos, hospitals, and offices

Buildings with reception or security need slightly different details than a private home.

For hotels, condos, and hospitals:

- give the **guest or patient name exactly as registered**
- include a **contact number** for handover
- if you do not know the room number, say clearly that flowers can be left at **reception** or a designated desk
- add any note the front desk should attach to the bouquet

For offices, name the company, floor, and who should receive the flowers—and say if the delivery must be face-to-face.

Drivers cannot wait indefinitely. Our standard expectation is about **15 minutes** after arrival or successful contact, with a maximum on-site wait described in the [delivery policy](/en/info/delivery-policy).

- confirm someone can accept the bouquet in the chosen time window
- for surprises, arrange a backup person at reception or next door
