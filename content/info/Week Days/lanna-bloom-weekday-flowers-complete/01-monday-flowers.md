# Monday Flowers in Thailand: Yellow Bouquet Meaning

> **Cursor instruction:** Lanna Bloom already has a Monday article using yellow as its main idea. Preserve the existing URL and content. Use this file to add missing metadata, internal links, FAQs and structured data rather than creating a duplicate page.

In Thailand, Monday is traditionally represented by yellow or cream and associated with the Moon. A bouquet using yellow roses, sunflowers, orchids or ivory flowers can therefore become a personal gift for someone born on Monday.

This color association belongs to a wider Thai weekday tradition influenced by Mahathaksa astrology. It is a cultural association rather than proof that one particular flower is officially assigned to Monday.

## Why Monday is yellow in Thailand

Thai cultural sources connect Monday with the Moon and yellow or cream. Yellow is also highly visible in modern Thai royal observances because King Bhumibol Adulyadej the Great and King Maha Vajiralongkorn were born on Mondays.

For flower gifting, the tradition can be interpreted through color rather than a single flower species.

## Suitable Monday flowers

- **Yellow roses:** warm and polished for birthdays, friendship or congratulations.
- **Sunflowers:** bright and energetic for cheerful surprises.
- **Yellow orchids:** elegant for formal gifts, offices or hotels.
- **Cream lilies:** graceful for family, anniversaries or recovery wishes.
- **Yellow-and-white mixed bouquets:** flexible for most recipients and occasions.

A soft yellow-and-cream palette often feels more refined than a completely bright yellow arrangement.

## Personalizing a Monday bouquet

Choose the recipient’s preferred style first. Use bright yellow for an expressive gift or cream and ivory for a quieter design. Add white flowers, greenery or pale pink details when a fully yellow bouquet feels too strong.

A card can say:

> “Yellow is the traditional Thai color for Monday, so this bouquet was chosen especially for your day of birth.”
