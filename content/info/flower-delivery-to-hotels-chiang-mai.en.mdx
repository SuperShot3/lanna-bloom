## What to prepare before you order

Hotels usually need a few clear details before they accept a delivery for a guest. Having these ready makes checkout smoother and reduces back-and-forth.

<ArticleChecklistBlock title="Essential details" variant="essential">

- **Guest full name** (as registered at the hotel)
- **Hotel name** and **full address**
- **Check-in date** (helpful if the guest has not arrived yet)
- **Room number** (if known—if not, front desk delivery is fine)
- **Guest phone number** (very important if the hotel needs to confirm)
- **Your preferred delivery date and time window**
- **Message card text** (optional but recommended for surprises)

</ArticleChecklistBlock>

<ArticleChecklistBlock title="Strongly recommended at checkout" variant="recommended">

- A **Google Maps link** to the hotel entrance or lobby pin
- **Driver notes**: building wing, reception name, “please call guest on arrival,” surprise timing, etc.

</ArticleChecklistBlock>

For homes and hard-to-find spots outside hotels, see [how to give a clear flower delivery address in Chiang Mai](/en/info/flower-delivery-address-chiang-mai).

If you are ordering from outside Thailand, double-check the time zone and share any surprise timing in the order notes—we can coordinate carefully when you tell us the plan upfront.

## How hotel flower delivery usually works

Every property handles deliveries slightly differently, but most follow a similar pattern.

<ArticleStepsBlock>

1. **You choose a bouquet** on our [catalog](/en/catalog) and complete checkout (or message us for a custom request).
2. **We prepare the flowers** for your selected date and time window.
3. **Our driver arrives at the hotel** and checks in at reception or the concierge desk.
4. **The hotel contacts the guest** or holds the bouquet until the guest collects it—depending on hotel policy and your instructions.

</ArticleStepsBlock>

Some hotels prefer to keep surprises discreet: flowers may be held at the front desk until the guest asks, or delivered to the room only after the guest confirms. If you want a specific approach, tell us in the order notes and we will do our best to match it.

## FAQ

### Can you deliver flowers directly to a hotel room?

Sometimes yes, but many hotels ask deliveries to go through reception or concierge first. Provide the guest name, hotel name, and phone number—we will follow the hotel’s preferred procedure.

### What if I do not know the room number yet?

That is common. Send the **guest’s full name**, **hotel name**, **check-in date**, and a **reachable phone number**. The hotel can usually match the guest and hold or forward the bouquet.

### Can I schedule delivery for when my partner checks in?

Yes—choose your preferred date and time window at checkout, then add a note such as “guest arriving around 15:00.” We will plan around that as closely as delivery routing allows.

### Do you deliver to resorts outside central Chiang Mai?

We serve Chiang Mai and nearby areas according to the zones shown at checkout. Add a Maps link for resorts in Mae Rim, Hang Dong, or San Sai so we can confirm coverage before you rely on a tight surprise window.

### Can I order from another country for a hotel in Chiang Mai?

Yes. Place the order online with the hotel as the delivery address, or read [what to expect when sending flowers from abroad](/en/info/birthday-flowers-chiang-mai-from-abroad).

### Is same-day delivery to hotels possible?

Same-day delivery may be available during working hours, depending on bouquet availability and location. Order as early as you can and share your timing in the notes.
